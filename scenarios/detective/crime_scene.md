# The Thornfield Gallery Murder

## Setting
The prestigious Thornfield Art Gallery hosted its annual gala last night. This morning, the gallery's owner **Marcus Thornfield** (age 58) was found dead in the Restoration Room, slumped over a work table. The room was locked from the inside.

## Victim
Marcus Thornfield — wealthy art dealer, known for sharp business practices. Recently announced plans to sell the gallery, angering several people close to him.

## Time of Death
Between 10:30 PM and 11:15 PM last night, during the gala's final hour.

## Suspects

### 1. Diana Voss (Gallery Director)
- 42, ran the gallery's daily operations for 12 years
- Would lose her position if the gallery sells
- Was seen arguing with Marcus at 9:45 PM
- Claims she was in the main hall from 10 PM onward

### 2. Leo Marchetti (Art Restorer)
- 35, freelance restorer who rented workspace in the gallery
- Had a key to the Restoration Room
- Owed Marcus $40,000 for a failed restoration job
- Claims he left the gala at 10 PM and went home

### 3. Petra Novak (Caterer)
- 29, ran the catering company for the gala
- Marcus's ex-partner, bitter breakup 6 months ago
- Had access to the kitchen adjacent to the Restoration Room
- Claims she was managing staff in the kitchen all evening

## The Puzzle
The Restoration Room was locked from the inside with a deadbolt. There's a small ventilation window (6 inches) that connects to the kitchen. The key to the deadbolt was found in Marcus's pocket.

The real killer used a method involving the ventilation system and the kitchen — but surface-level clues point elsewhere.
